# Go-Live Brief — context for the planning session

Written 20 Jul 2026 by Claude (from the consult-ops session at ~/projects). Purpose: give this session the strategic context that lives outside this repo.

## Why sitefile is going live

Derian is transitioning from permanent employment (Associate Director, Turner & Townsend) to day-rate consultancy — the consultancy is a **bridge**; building/monetizing his own tools is the **destination**. He has explicitly committed (20 Jul 2026): "it's on me to actually use them and go live with the monetization ones such as sitefile."

sitefile = contractor-side tool for generating weekly/monthly construction reports to the client side. Built by Derian; sits at the heart of his professional credibility story.

## What go-live unlocks beyond revenue

- **Brand content trigger**: his LinkedIn brand system (see ~/projects/consult-ops, brand plan in docs/) has sitefile go-live wired as a triggered flagship post — "contractors on a live programme now report through a tool I built." Do not publicise before genuinely live.
- **Capability-statement proof point** for his consultancy positioning ("builder embedded in infrastructure").

## Constraints the plan must respect

1. **Employer IP/optics check FIRST**: the natural pilot is contractors on his own T&T commission (Stansted). Before any deployment into his employer's delivery environment, he must review his T&T contract's IP / outside-business-interest clauses and, if needed, have the open conversation. A personally-owned tool discovered inside an employer programme without clearance would contaminate the asset. This is a gating item, not a footnote.
2. **Zero departure signals**: nothing about the go-live or its marketing may hint at his consultancy transition (active constraint until he resigns/flips).
3. **His time budget is thin**: day job + transition admin + LinkedIn routines (daily 15 min + Sunday 90 min). The go-live plan should be scoped for the hours that actually exist.
4. Naming: once live, sitefile CAN be named publicly in his content (pre-live, his voice guide keeps tools generic).

## Suggested planning agenda

1. Current state audit: what works end-to-end, what's demo-only, gaps to "a real contractor uses it unsupervised".
2. Define "live": one pilot contractor on one real programme? Self-serve? Paid or free pilot?
3. The IP question (above) — resolve before pilot selection.
4. Pilot candidate + success criteria + date.
5. Monetization model sketch (defer detail; pilot learning first).
